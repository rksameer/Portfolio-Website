Research Experience

2023–now
Theory & Simulations of Polymer Melt Crystallization
Columbia University · Advisor: Prof. Sanat K. Kumar
Crystallization of a subcooled polymer melt following primary nucleation events is modeled using non-equilibrium thermodynamics at mesoscale length and time scales. Yields a pair of nonlinear differential equations (analogous to phase-field models): a thermal energy balance coupled to a rate law for melt-to-solid conversion. Codes for solving non-linear PDEs using implicit, explicit, and semi-implicit finite-difference methods written in C and Python.
Phase-Field Modeling Non-equilibrium Thermodynamics Polymer Crystallization C / Python Finite Difference Methods
2023–now
Directional Crystallization of Semicrystalline Polymers
Columbia University · Collaborators: UPV/EHU Spain · University of Vermont
Directional crystallization is a promising pathway to reprocess end-of-life multi-layered plastics. This novel processing protocol enables precise engineering of micro-scale structure with direct consequences on macro-scale mechanical, electrical, and magnetic properties. Explored through a combination of SAXS, DSC, TEM, DMA, and GPC experiments.
SAXS / WAXS DSC TEM GPC Plastic Upcycling DMA
2021–23
Modeling of Self-assembly in Polymer Nanocomposites
IIT Madras · Thesis Advisor: Prof. Ethayaraja Mani · Collaborator: Prof. Sanat K. Kumar
Used meso-scale simulations to study the role of nanoparticle dispersion on mechanical properties in polymer nanocomposites. Performed Coarse-Grained Molecular Dynamics (CG-MD) using LAMMPS, and used Python, VMD, and C for post-processing. Work published in ACS Macromolecules (Q1 journal).
LAMMPS CG-MD Polymer Nanocomposites VMD Rheology
